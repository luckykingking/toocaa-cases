1) TL;DR

Laser-engraved QR codes must meet two requirements: sufficient contrast between engraved and non-engraved areas, and minimum module size (≥0.5mm per QR module). Use high error correction (Level H = 30%), test at the expected scanning distance, and choose high-contrast materials like anodized aluminum or painted wood. For signage text, focus on clean edges using optimized speed/power and air assist. This page covers the complete workflow from QR generation to final scan testing.

3) HowTo: Engrave a scannable QR code on an anodized aluminum tag

This workflow produces a high-contrast, scannable QR code every time.

StepWhat you doWhy
1Generate a QR code with Error Correction Level H (30%) and export as SVG or high-res PNG (≥300 DPI).Level H tolerates minor engraving imperfections and still scans reliably.
2Size the QR so each module is ≥0.5mm. For a V2 QR (25×25), that's ≥12.5mm total. Larger is better.Below 0.5mm, module edges blur together and scanners fail.
3Import into TOOCAA Studio. Place the QR and add a quiet zone (≥4 modules of blank space around the QR).The quiet zone helps scanners detect the QR boundary.
4Place anodized aluminum tag on honeycomb bed. Set focus. Enable air assist.Anodized aluminum gives the best contrast: laser removes anodization to reveal bright metal.
5Engrave at 70% power / 3000 mm/min / 1 pass. Adjust based on array test results for your specific material.Goal: clean removal of the anodized layer without over-engraving the metal beneath.
6Scan test: try scanning with 3 different phones from 15cm, 30cm, and 60cm distances.Real-world validation catches issues that visual inspection misses.
Contrast tip: If scanning fails, try inverting the QR (engrave the background, leave modules raised). This sometimes improves contrast depending on the material. Array Test CSV

4) Common questions

From sign makers, restaurant owners, and product manufacturers.

What is the minimum QR code size for laser engraving?

Each QR module should be at least 0.5mm. For a Version 2 QR (25×25 modules), minimum total size is 12.5×12.5mm. Test with your phone camera from the expected scanning distance. Larger codes are always more reliable.

My laser-engraved QR code won't scan — what went wrong?

Common causes: insufficient contrast, modules too small, or charring that blurs edges. Increase QR size, optimize power/speed for clean contrast, and set error correction to Level H (30%).

Which material gives the best QR code contrast?

Anodized aluminum gives the highest contrast (white mark on dark surface). Painted/lacquered wood also works well. Avoid raw light-colored wood as the contrast is often too low for reliable scanning.

Can laser-engraved signs be used outdoors?

Yes, with the right material. Anodized aluminum and stainless steel are weather-resistant. For wooden signs, apply a UV-resistant clear coat after engraving. Acrylic works outdoors but may yellow in direct sunlight.
